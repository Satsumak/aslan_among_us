ROLES

CREWMATE

GOALS

Starting screen for crewmates

The primary goal of a Crewmate is to complete all tasks while not being killed by the Impostor, with the secondary goal being Finding the Impostor, and kicking them off the ship. Crewmates who have been killed by an Impostor become ghosts. The Crewmates are assigned a variety of tasks, ranging from fixing color-coded wiring, uploading data to the admin, refueling ship engines, memorizing patterns, as well as many more tasks not listed here.

ABILTIES
REPORT

Visual of report button

A Crewmate can use the report button to report a dead body, and bring the entire crew into a meeting to discuss who the Impostor may be. Impostors also have this ability.

USE

A Crewmate can use the 'Use' button for a variety of interactions. The most common is for activating and completing tasks. Crewmates can also use this button to manually call an emergency meeting (if the host allows for it), check security cameras, check doorlogs, or to view the admin map. Impostors also have this button, but cannot complete tasks with it.

DEATH

Dead character in ghost form

When a crew member is killed by the Impostor, their body is dropped to the ground, completely broken in half with a bone sticking out, and a ghost will appear from the body. In this state a Crewmate can move freely through the walls, without having to cross an entire corridor to reach the place and can do tasks, however, the only disadvantages are that their messages in the chats do not appear to the remaining survivors, they cannot start an emergency meeting, or resolve sabotages. Ghosts can also use the chat feature outside of meetings to chat with other ghosts. The corpse of a Crewmate disappears after it is reported, as well as if someone reports another body or an emergency meeting is called. If the Crewmate has a pet, it will stay on the spot they have died. This can prove to be decisive evidence to convict the Impostor posing as a Crewmate, if used wisely.

IMPOSTOR

GOALS

The primary goal of the Impostor is to kill enough Crewmates to have an equal amount of impostor(s) and crewmates before all tasks are completed, and to have their true identity remain hidden.

The Impostor cannot complete any tasks, however, they are given a task list as general guidance on how to feign the appearance of a Crewmate. Impostors have the ability to perform various acts of sabotage in order to force Crewmates into unfortunate scenarios.

Impostor starting screen

Depending on game settings, there can be up to three Impostors in a game. The chance of a player being an Impostor is 100 multiplied by the number of impostors divided by the number of players, for example: in a one-Impostor lobby of 2, each player has a 50% chance of being an Impostor, whereas on a lobby of 4, each player has a 25% chance, and in a lobby of 10, each player has a 10% chance, etc. This chance does not change regardless of your record as an Impostor or Crewmate; it is consistently random between all members of the lobby.

EJECTIONS

Impostor being ejected

Impostors, if discovered, will be ejected off the map and killed in the process. This is depicted through various scenes depending on the current map: if playing on The Skeld, the Impostor is ejected off the airlock and into space, on MIRA HQ they are pushed off the station to fall to their death, and on Polus, they are thrown into a lava pool. A difference between the Crewmate ejection animation and the Imposter ejection animation is the imposter will have a thumbs up when falling in the lava, while a crewmate will stick their hand out, like reaching for help. The Imposter giving a thumbs up may be a reference to Terminator 2. If there are still other Impostors left after one is ejected, that Impostor will become a ghost like the crew would. However if no Impostors remain alive, the crew wins immediately. As a ghost, Impostors can continue to sabotage the map to help their fellow Impostors win.

ABILITES
KILL

An Impostor has the ability to kill a Crewmate. Killing a Crewmate turns them into a ghost, and leaves a dead body in the spot they were killed at. Kills have a cooldown of 10-60 seconds, and a kill distance of either Short, Normal, and Long, depending on game settings.

Body of crewmate after being killed

In the view of the Impostor, when killing someone, the player's body is broken in half, where after a few seconds, they fall to the ground, exposing their bone, however, blood does not appear in the animation. In the Crewmate victim's view, while his body falls to the ground, an animation is shown, which can appear in different ways, either the crew member is shot, stabbed, impaled, or had their neck snapped.

The kill cooldown comes to a halt/stops if you are accessing tools or fixing crises, such as being inside a vent, looking through security cameras, accessing the admin map, looking through logs or fixing oxygen/lights/reactor.

The kill cooldown is shortened to 10 seconds on the first round before any meetings, and will be set to the normal cooldown time after a body has been reported or an emergency meeting is called.

The kill ability also forces the Imposter to move instantaneously to the location of the Crewmate, essentially teleporting there leaving a faint trail of their presence in their previous location.

SABOTAGE

Sabotage button for impostors

An Impostor has the ability to Sabotage, which compromises an aspect of the map's machinery, creating a temporary problem that Crewmates must either wait out, ignore, or fix. Previously, Impostors could not solve sabotage, which made discovering them easier, but nowadays they are able to; allowing an Impostor to fix their own sabotage to avoid suspicion. Sabotage can also be done as a ghost.

The sabotage cooldown comes to a halt/stops if you are accessing tasks/tools such as being inside a vent, looking through security cameras, accessing the admin map, looking through logs, or fixing oxygen/lights/reactor.

The reactor meltdown and O2 shutdown are events that, if not fixed, will result in a win for the Impostor. Sabotaging lights or communications will not result in a win, but will make it harder for crewmates to do their tasks until fixed by compromising their vision or task list respectively.

The communications sabotage will make finding their tasks much harder, forcing them to fix communications; however, it can be ignored. A communications sabotage will also disable functions such as Admin, Doorlog, Vitals, and Security Cameras for all players, regardless of whether they are an impostor or crewmate.

Sabotaging map

The lights sabotage will lower the crewmates' vision, it often plays with their eyes and makes them suspect one another when a nearby crewmate is killed. This can be ignored; however it is not recommended due to the higher chance of not finding the dead body of crewmates, and not being able to see nearby players who are potential Impostors.

The Impostor can Sabotage doors, which closes them for 10 seconds. This Sabotage has a 17-second cooldown, which begins when the door is initially sabotaged; meaning that after the door opens, the Impostor will have to wait 7 seconds before closing it again. Sabotage cannot be accessed beside vents.

All sabotage cooldowns are shared between all Impostors, so if you sabotage something you may hamper your teammate. All sabotage cooldowns besides doors are the same, so by sabotaging one thing you activate a cooldown for all other sabotages, except for the doors.

VENT

Vent button for impostors

The Impostor can hide in vents. Vents allow the Impostor to either hide or quickly move around the map. When close enough to a vent, the Sabotage button will be replaced with the vent button. However, hiding in the vent stops your kill cooldown and sabotage cooldown until you exit the vent.

REPORT

Like the crew, Impostors can use this ability to report dead bodies anywhere. This is also a form of disguise whose purpose is to avoid suspicion. When reporting, this opens up the moment of discussion about who the Impostor is

USE

Impostors also have this ability that allows them to interact with other objects, the only ones being the emergency button, the administration map, security cameras, sensors, and the vitality machine. This is not true for people playing on mobile.
